## Abstract

GrepCoin (GREP) introduces a decentralized ecosystem designed to revolutionize how hobby projects and indie games are funded, developed, and monetized. By creating a unified token economy that connects creators, players, and supporters, GrepCoin eliminates traditional barriers to entry for independent developers while providing sustainable funding mechanisms and genuine community ownership.

The platform addresses critical gaps in the current landscape: fragmented funding sources, high platform fees, lack of cross-project interoperability, and limited creator autonomy. Through blockchain technology, smart contracts, and community governance, GrepCoin establishes a self-sustaining ecosystem where value flows directly between participants without extractive intermediaries.

With a fixed supply of 500 million tokens, carefully designed tokenomics, and multiple utility drivers, GrepCoin aims to become the standard currency for indie game economies and hobby project funding worldwide.

## Introduction

### The Rise of Independent Creation

The past decade has witnessed an explosion of independent game development and hobby projects. Tools like Unity, Unreal Engine, and Godot have democratized game creation. Platforms like itch.io, Game Jolt, and indie showcases have provided distribution channels. Yet despite these advances, fundamental economic challenges persist.

Independent creators face a paradox: never has it been easier to build, yet never has it been harder to sustain. The attention economy rewards established players, funding mechanisms extract significant value, and creators often struggle to convert passion into sustainable livelihoods.

### Why Blockchain?

Blockchain technology offers unique properties that align with the needs of independent creators:

- **Disintermediation**: Direct value transfer without platform rent-seeking
- **Transparency**: Open, auditable transactions and fund usage
- **Programmability**: Smart contracts enable novel funding and reward mechanisms
- **Community Ownership**: Token holders become genuine stakeholders
- **Interoperability**: Assets and currency can flow across project boundaries
- **Permanence**: Projects and achievements persist independent of any company

GrepCoin harnesses these properties to build infrastructure specifically designed for the indie and hobby project ecosystem.

## Problem Statement

### 1. The Funding Gap

Traditional funding mechanisms fail independent creators:

| Funding Source | Problems |
|----------------|----------|
| **Kickstarter/Crowdfunding** | High fees (8-10%), all-or-nothing risk, no ongoing support |
| **Publisher Deals** | Loss of creative control, unfavorable revenue splits |
| **Self-Funding** | Limited resources, unsustainable for most creators |
| **Grants** | Highly competitive, bureaucratic, inconsistent |
| **Early Access** | Pressure to deliver before ready, reputation risk |

Creators often piece together multiple sources, each extracting fees and imposing constraints. The average indie game developer earns below minimum wage for their efforts.

### 2. Platform Extraction

Digital storefronts and platforms capture disproportionate value:

- Steam: 30% revenue share
- Apple/Google: 30% revenue share
- Payment processors: 3-5% per transaction
- Crowdfunding platforms: 5-10% + payment fees

A creator might lose 40-50% of gross revenue before receiving anything. This extraction is particularly devastating for small-scale projects with thin margins.

### 3. Fragmented Economies

Each game and platform operates as an isolated economic island:

- Virtual currencies don't transfer between games
- Player investments are locked within single titles
- Cross-promotion is difficult and inefficient
- Network effects don't compound across projects

This fragmentation limits growth potential and reduces player investment in the broader ecosystem.

### 4. Misaligned Incentives

Traditional models create adversarial relationships:

- Platforms profit regardless of creator success
- Investors seek returns that may conflict with creative vision
- Players have no stake in project success beyond entertainment value
- Communities lack formal voice in project direction

Without aligned incentives, the ecosystem optimizes for extraction rather than creation.

### 5. Discoverability Crisis

With thousands of games releasing annually, visibility is a primary challenge:

- Marketing budgets determine success more than quality
- Algorithmic recommendations favor established titles
- Organic discovery mechanisms are inadequate
- Small creators struggle to reach their audience

## Technology Stack

### Blockchain Selection Criteria

GrepCoin requires a blockchain that provides:

1. **Low Transaction Costs**: Micro-transactions must be viable
2. **High Throughput**: Gaming requires responsive transactions
3. **Strong Security**: Protecting user assets is paramount
4. **Developer Ecosystem**: Rich tooling and documentation
5. **Interoperability**: Bridge to major chains
6. **Sustainability**: Long-term viability and development

### Recommended Architecture

**Primary Chain**: Ethereum Layer 2 (Base or Arbitrum)

**Rationale:**
- Ethereum security guarantees
- Sub-cent transaction costs
- 2-4 second finality
- EVM compatibility for rich tooling
- Growing ecosystem and adoption

- Standard ERC-20 implementation

- Permit for gasless approvals

- Manages team, advisor, and investor vesting
- Cliff and linear release logic
- Revocation capability for unvested tokens
- Beneficiary management

#### StakingPool.sol
- Multi-tier staking with time locks
- Reward calculation and distribution
- Emergency withdrawal (with penalty)
- Compound functionality

#### LaunchpadEscrow.sol
- Project registration and verification
- Milestone definition and tracking
- Fund collection and release
- Refund logic for failed projects

#### Marketplace.sol
- Order book or AMM for asset trading
- Fee collection and distribution
- Royalty enforcement

- Proposal creation and voting
- Timelock execution

- Delegation support

1. **Phase 1 (Year 1)**: Core team leadership with community input

### Governance Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Proposal Threshold | 100,000 GREP (0.02%) |
| Quorum | 10% of staked supply |
| Voting Period | 5 days |
| Timelock Delay | 48 hours |
| Passing Threshold | 66% approval |

### Proposal Lifecycle

1. **Ideation**: Community discussion on forum
2. **Temperature Check**: Informal poll to gauge interest
3. **Formal Proposal**: On-chain submission with required stake
4. **Review Period**: 2 days for community analysis
5. **Voting Period**: 5 days of token-weighted voting
6. **Timelock**: 48 hours before execution (allows exit)
7. **Execution**: Automatic or manual trigger

### Council Structure (Phase 2+)

**Core Council (5 seats)**
- 2 elected by token holders
- 2 appointed by founding team
- 1 elected by active creators

**Responsibilities:**
- Emergency response
- Proposal screening
- Conflict resolution
- Strategic guidance

**Limitations:**
- Cannot override token holder votes
- Subject to recall by token holders
- Term limits (1 year, max 2 consecutive)
